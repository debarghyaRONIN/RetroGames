POKEMON

Pokémon, developed by Game Freak, began in 1996 with Pokémon Red and Green for the Game Boy. Players assume the role of trainers, capturing and training creatures called Pokémon to battle other trainers and become the Pokémon Champion. The series' success led to various generations, introducing new regions, Pokémon, and mechanics, like breeding and abilities.

In 1998, the franchise expanded to TV, movies, and trading card games, becoming a global phenomenon. Notable entries include Pokémon Gold and Silver, introducing day-night cycles, and Pokémon Ruby and Sapphire with double battles. The augmented reality game, Pokémon GO, took the world by storm in 2016. The series continues to thrive with constant updates, spin-offs, and a dedicated fanbase worldwide.
